Selection of my works

During my PhD program, I have worked on multiple subjects ranging from conducting behavioral experiments to computational modeling. My first year's project was about understanding how location and object identities are stored in memory during fast presentations. The results are reported in the following paper:

Hedayati, S., Wyble, B. (2020). Memories of Visual Events Can Be Formed without Specific Spatial Coordinates.Journal of Cognition, 3(1), 13. Access Paper. MATLAB codes and data are available at the OSF Link.

The above research was more experimental. In my second year, my focus shifted towards computational modeling. I designed a tool using an MCMC search algorithm, so researchers can better undertand attentional blink findings. The tool maps the model's parameters into meaningful cognitive constructs. The results are reported here:

Hedayati, S., Wyble, B. (2021). How to interpret attentional blink findings? A practical MCMC tool to assess the attentional blink with the eSTST model. Manuscript under review (PsyArXiv) . The codes are available here.

My major research began in my third year. I designed a computational model of working memory that meets several behavioral and neural benchmarks. Using advances in deep learning we tried to explain the relationship between the working memory and visual knowledge. The results are reported here:

Hedayati, S., O'Donnell, R., Wyble, B. (2021). The Memory for Latent Representations: An Account of Working Memory that Builds on Visual Knowledge for Efficient and Detailed Visual Representations. Manuscript under review (bioRxiv) . The python codes are available in my GitHub page.

My research is not limited to computational modeling of cognition. Due to my interest in more applied domains, specifically in system design, my collaborator and I conducted a meta-analysis study to determine the relationship between perceptual fidelity and transfer in training simulators.

Doozandeh, P., & Hedayati, S. (2021). The effect of fidelity on transfer: A meta-analysis. Manuscript under review.

I have given several talks in Vision Science Society (VSS conference) , Society for Mathematical Psychology and Neuromatch Academy . I also have several teaching experiences, including being a lab instructor of psychological method course. For more details about my resume please contact my email.

What is the needed level of fidelity for sucessful training? To answer this question, My colleage and I conducted a mixed method study to undertand how much level of realism is needed to be incorporated in training simulators for successful transfer in the real target system.
